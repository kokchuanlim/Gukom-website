Proprietary Technology

Built for the unique challenges of Southeast Asian due diligence.

Four original technical components addressing structural failures that existing tools cannot solve.

Component 01

Heterogeneous Graph Neural Network (HGNN)

Fuses ownership graph nodes — entities, directors, shareholders, beneficial owners — with transaction flow nodes into a single heterogeneous structure. Relation-specific message passing with separate attention heads detects circular shareholding, nominee director arrangements, and synthetic revenue flows that are invisible to tabular or text-only models.

Fraud Detection
Component 02

Federated GNN Training Under Data Sovereignty

The global corporate graph is divided into country-specific subgraphs trained locally. Only anonymised, differentially-private gradient updates — never raw entity records — are transmitted to a Singapore-based aggregation server. Cross-border entity relationships are represented via hashed privacy-preserving node anchors. A formal ε-DP budget provides guarantees auditable by local regulators.

Primary Technical Novelty
Component 03

Southeast Asia-Specific Legal Language Model

Standard multilingual models achieve below 60% entity extraction accuracy on SEA legal texts. Gukom constructs a domain-specific annotated corpus across five jurisdictions and applies parameter-efficient LoRA fine-tuning — targeting >85% F1 on named entity recognition. An entity schema standardisation layer normalises outputs across heterogeneous legal systems.

Legal NLP · LoRA Fine-Tuning
Component 04

Bayesian-HMM Timeline Reconstruction Engine

Corporate registries routinely contain conflicting records — a tax authority may record an ownership change 6–18 months before the business registry. Our engine models entity state transitions using a Hidden Markov Model, treating each registry as a probabilistic observation source. Bayesian evidence fusion reconstructs the most probable ownership sequence with confidence-scored timelines.

Multi-Registry Reconciliation
